Cirrus Clouds

Altitude


Cirrus clouds usally form anywhere in between 4,000 and 20,000 meters (13,000 and 66,000 feet) (4 km and 20 km) or higher above sea level

Apearance


Cirrus cloud usally have white stripes and are spread-out

Formation


Cirrus clouds are usually formed as warm, dry air rises, causing water vapor to under go deposition (going to solid straight from a gas without turning into liquid) onto rocky or metallic dust particles at high altitudes.
